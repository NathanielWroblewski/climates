Visualizing Climate

Observations from the conterminous United States for the year 2017

When choosing a place to live, people often consider climate, inter alia, but as the number of options grows, the degrees of difference lessen, trade-offs become multi-dimensional, and heuristically comparing climates becomes quite challenging.

The projection above reveals interesting regional patterns, but it is less suited for determining the degree of difference. For a comparison of climate features by degree, a mult-dimensional scatter plot may be more useful. Figure two depicts how temperature variance and rainfall vary with average temperature. To find consistently hot and dry locations in figure two, zoom-in on the bottom-rightmost corner where rainfall is zero, temperatures are highest, and variance is at a minimum.
